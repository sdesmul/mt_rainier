Caring for your Sphynx

Curious about the Sphynx breed? Watch this Video from Cats 101

Nutrition

Sphynx have a high metabolism then cats with hair. This means that a Sphynx requires more food. It is best to feed a high quality food to avoid fillers, and harmful chemicals. The general health of your cat will be better, for longer.

Indoor Only

Sphynx cats do best indoors. They are susceptible to sunburns when outside. Sphynx are most comfortable at 70 degrees.

Baths

Sphynx need weekly baths to wash off the oil that builds up. Using a delicate baby shampoo is recommended.

Nail trimming

Sphynx need their nails clipped every two weeks. Only the sharp end of the claw can be cut off, otherwise you will damage the blood vessels in their claws.

Ear Cleaning

Sphynx need their ears cleaned once a week, many owners do that right after their cats baths. A dark wax builds up that you clean carefully with q-tips or cotton balls.

Brushing Teeth

Brushing a Sphynx teeth is a crucial part of their health. Food build up creates plaque which causes gum disease. Ideally you would brush your Sphynx teeth every day. DO NOT use toothpaste for humans, it is toxic to cats. There is cat specific toothpaste If you do not know how to start a dental regime with your cat watch the following video Cat Dental Care

Eye Care

Since Sphynx are hairless they lack eyelashes. Because of this Sphynx can get goopy eyes. Eyes should be carefully cleaned, consult vet on how to clean them.
